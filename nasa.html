NASA Aeronautics Design Challenge

Goldeneye claimed Honours in the 2016-17 NASA Advanced Air Vehicles Symposium, with its design submission for a commercial supersonic aircraft. Goldeneye AB1, after which the group was named, features a novel variable-geometry wing designed to deliver high lift in the subsonic and supersonic regimes. As the group's pilot project, Goldeneye AB1 competed against 26 different teams, most of whom came from schools with specific Aerospace Engineering departments, with several submitting capstone undergraduate projects as their entries.

The group was invited to present their paper during the winner's symposium at NASA's Langley Research Center from September 25th-26th. Read our whitepaper for more.
